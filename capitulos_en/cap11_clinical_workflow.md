# Chapter 10 — Clinical Workflow: From Map to Ring

> *Part III — From Theory to Clinical Practice*

---

## 10.1 Introduction

This chapter presents the AVBC clinical protocol as a sequential decision tree, distilling the trimodal assessment (O, T, B) into practical steps. The protocol is additive: it uses existing clinical data reorganized within a mechanical framework.

> [!NOTE]
> **AVBC Preoperative Checklist:**
> - `[ ]` Manifest and cycloplegic refraction
> - `[ ]` Scheimpflug tomography with anterior/posterior maps
> - `[ ]` MNA calculated
> - `[ ]` Wavefront aberrometry (6 mm pupil)
> - `[ ]` Local pachymetry at implant meridian (for RT_max)
> - `[ ]` O, T, B Modules classified
> - `[ ]` Final prescription verified against RT_max

---

## 10.2 Preoperative Sequence

**Step 1:** Standard ophthalmologic exam (BCVA, refraction, slit lamp, pachymetry, IOP).
**Step 2:** Corneal tomography → Morphology classification + MNA determination.
**Step 3:** Wavefront aberrometry → O Module input.
**Step 4:** Biomechanical assessment (Corvis ST, ORA, Brillouin if available).

---

## 10.3–10.5 Module Classifications

### O Module
- **O+** (≥ 3 favorable): Proceed with confidence.
- **O~** (mixed): Partial functional improvement expected.
- **O−** (≥ 3 unfavorable): Topographic management only.

### T Module
Classify morphology → Determine MNA → Record |MNA − K-steep|.

### B Module Decision Tree
1. Flattening needed → **VR** → Increase **thickness**
2. Regularization needed → **VT** → Increase **arc length**
3. Apex repositioning needed → **Vτ** → **Asymmetric ring**
4. Stabilization needed → CXL first, then ICRS

---

## 10.6 Ring Prescription

### VR-Dominant
Thickness 250–400 μm, arc 120°–160°, symmetric, 70–80% depth, K-steep axis.

### VT-Dominant
Thickness 150–250 μm, arc 210°–320°, symmetric, 70–75% depth, MNA axis.

### Vτ-Dominant
Progressive 300→150 μm, arc 160°–210°, asymmetric, 70–80% depth, MNA axis.

### Thickness Law
$$RT_{max} \le CT \times 0.60$$

Profile-specific: Triangular = 0.60; Ferrara HM = 0.55; Rounded = 0.60.

> [!CAUTION]
> **Do Not Reverse the Progressive Ring!** The thick end goes on the **same side as the cone**.

---

## 10.7 Clinical Examples

### Example 1: Central Symmetric (VR Dominant)
28M, K-steep 49.5, central oval, MNA ≈ K-steep → Ferrara 250 μm, 160° arc, symmetric. **Nomogram agrees.**

### Example 2: Paracentral with MNA Divergence (VT + Vτ)
32F, K-steep 52.0, paracentral crescent, |MNA − K-steep| = 5° → Progressive 250→150 μm, 210° arc. **Nomogram disagrees on axis and arc.**

---

## 10.8 Postoperative Feedback Loop

**At 3–6 months:**
- **CI_R** = ΔK_induced / ΔK_predicted
- **VT-ratio** = ΔCyl_induced / ΔCyl_expected
- **Vτ-ratio** = Migration_observed / Migration_expected

**Personal Calibration Curve:** Over N cases, mean CI_R = surgeon's personal factor (analogous to IOL A constant).

---

## 10.9 Summary
- Sequential O → T → B workflow
- MNA assessment changes plan in ~40% of cases
- Thickness → VR, Arc → VT, Asymmetry → Vτ, Depth → universal amplifier
- Personal CI_R calibration enables continuous improvement
