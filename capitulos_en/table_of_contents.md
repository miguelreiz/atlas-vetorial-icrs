### Pedagogical Logic

The six-part structure follows the didactic principle **"Problem → Foundation → Solution → Practice → Proof"**:

1. **Part I** answers *"Why do I need this?"* — establishes the biomechanical basis and demonstrates nomogram limitations.
2. **Part II** answers *"What is AVBC?"* — presents the three biomechanical vectors and the Alpins parallel.
3. **Part III** answers *"How do I use this in the clinic?"* — from classification to clinical workflow with real cases.
4. **Part IV** answers *"What evidence exists?"* — complete computational validation.
5. **Part V** answers *"What are the limits and the future?"* — honest limitations, software platform, and conclusive vision.
