## 📖 INSTRUCTIONAL CONTENT

### Introduction

This chapter presents 4 representative clinical cases — one for each main phenotype. In each case, we apply the vector nomogram (Ch. 9) step by step and analyze the outcome using vectorial language.

The goal is twofold: (1) to train vectorial thinking and (2) to demonstrate that different cones require radically different strategies.

---

### Case 1: Central Cone (Nipple) — The Classic

**Patient:** Female, 32 years old. Moderate keratoconus OD.

| Data | Value |
|------|-------|
| K-max | 52.4 D (central) |
| K1/K2 | 44.8 / 48.2 D (Δ 3.4 D @ 85°) |
| Coma V | 0.3 μm RMS |
| Min Pachymetry | 478 μm |
| Apex | Centered (~0.4 mm from pupillary center) |

**Vectorial Reasoning:**
1. **Phenotype:** Nipple (central apex, low coma, small cone)
2. **Hierarchy:** VR >>> VT > Vτ (dispensable)
3. **Prescription:** 2 symmetric segments of 250 μm, 150° arc, axis @ 85°
4. **Expected Resultant Vector:** High VR, moderate VT, Vτ ≈ 0

**Post-operative Result (6 months):**

| Parameter | Pre | Post | Δ |
|-----------|-----|-----|---|
| K-max | 52.4 D | 47.1 D | −5.3 D |
| Cylinder | 3.4 D | 1.8 D | −1.6 D |
| Coma V | 0.3 μm | 0.2 μm | −0.1 μm |
| BSCVA | 20/40 | 20/25 | +3 lines |

**Vectorial Analysis:** High Resultant Spherical Vector. VR dominated (optimal ΔK). VT contributed (reasonable cylindrical reduction). Vτ irrelevant (coma was already low). Result consistent with prediction.

### Case 2: Oval Cone (Sagging) — The Torque Challenge

**Patient:** Male, 25 years old. Advanced keratoconus OS.

| Data | Value |
|------|-------|
| K-max | 58.7 D (inferior paracentral) |
| K1/K2 | 45.2 / 52.1 D (Δ 6.9 D @ 72°) |
| Coma V | 2.4 μm RMS |
| Min Pachymetry | 428 μm |
| Apex | Displaced 2.8 mm inferiorly |

**Vectorial Reasoning:**
1. **Phenotype:** Oval/Sagging (inferior apex, very high coma)
2. **Hierarchy:** Vτ >>> VComa >> VR
3. **Prescription:** Inferior progressive segment 300 μm (thick end under apex) + superior symmetric segment 200 μm. Axis @ 72° with 12° adjustment for coma.
4. **Expected Resultant Vector:** High Vτ (centralization), moderate VR, improving VComa

**Post-operative Result (6 months):**

| Parameter | Pre | Post | Δ |
|-----------|-----|-----|---|
| K-max | 58.7 D | 51.8 D | −6.9 D |
| Cylinder | 6.9 D | 3.2 D | −3.7 D |
| Coma V | 2.4 μm | 0.8 μm | −1.6 μm (−67%) |
| BSCVA | 20/60 | 20/30 | +5 lines |

**Vectorial Analysis:** Excellent Resultant Spherical Vector. Vτ dominated — the apex migrated 1.9 mm towards the center (VComa dropped 67%). VR also contributed (good ΔK). The asymmetric strategy was crucial — a symmetric ring would have flattened but would NOT have moved the apex.

### Case 3: Globus Cone — Brute Force

**Patient:** Male, 38 years old. Severe keratoconus OD.

| Data | Value |
|------|-------|
| K-max | 64.2 D (central-paracentral) |
| K1/K2 | 52.8 / 58.4 D (diffuse) |
| Coma V | 1.1 μm RMS |
| Min Pachymetry | 412 μm |
| Apex | Slightly inferior, but extensive cone |

**Vectorial Reasoning:**
1. **Phenotype:** Globus (large cone, high curvature in all meridians)
2. **Hierarchy:** Maximum VR >>> VT > Vτ
3. **Prescription:** 2 symmetric segments of 350 μm (maximum thickness), 210° arc, axis aligned to K-steep
4. **Expected Resultant Vector:** Very high VR, high VT (long arc), Vτ ≈ 0

**Post-operative Result (6 months):**

| Parameter | Pre | Post | Δ |
|-----------|-----|-----|---|
| K-max | 64.2 D | 54.6 D | −9.6 D |
| Cylinder | 5.6 D | 3.1 D | −2.5 D |
| Coma V | 1.1 μm | 0.7 μm | −0.4 μm |
| BSCVA | 20/80 | 20/40 | +4 lines |

**Vectorial Analysis:** Moderate-high Resultant Spherical Vector. Massive VR (ΔK of almost 10 D). VT contributed (cylindrical reduction). Coma improved little because the strategy prioritized flattening, not centralization — correct for this phenotype. Limitation: even with ΔK of ~10 D, residual K-max of 54.6 D is still elevated. Possible candidate for a second stage (reintervention or CXL).

### Case 4: The Error — Symmetric Ring in Oval Cone

**Patient:** Female, 27 years old. Keratoconus OD (oval cone).

| Data | Value |
|------|-------|
| K-max | 55.8 D (inferior paracentral) |
| Coma V | 1.9 μm RMS |
| Apex | Displaced 2.3 mm inferiorly |

**THE ERROR:** Surgeon used 2 symmetric segments of 250 μm (nipple cone strategy).

**Result (6 months):**

| Parameter | Pre | Post | Δ |
|-----------|-----|-----|---|
| K-max | 55.8 D | 50.2 D | −5.6 D (good VR) |
| Coma V | 1.9 μm | 1.7 μm | −0.2 μm (terrible!) |
| Satisfaction | — | Low | "Same distortion" |

**Error Analysis:** VR worked (K-max dropped). But Vτ ≈ 0 (symmetric ring). The apex did NOT move. Coma remained high. The patient improved in numbers but still sees "with a comet tail."

**Correction:** Exchange inferior segment for progressive → generate Vτ → centralize apex.

> **Lesson:** Good numbers (K-max) + high coma = bad Resultant Spherical Vector = dissatisfied patient.

### Summary of the 4 Cases

| Case | Phenotype | Strategy | Dominant Vector | Outcome |
|------|----------|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| 1 | Nipple | Thick symmetric | VR | Excellent |
| 2 | Oval | Progressive asymmetric | Vτ | Excellent |
| 3 | Globus | Double max symmetric | VR (brute force) | Good |
| 4 | **Oval + Wrong ring** | **Symmetric (error)** | **VR without Vτ** | **Suboptimal** |
