## 📖 INSTRUCTIONAL CONTENT

### Introduction

This chapter is a **pure visual atlas**. Minimal text, mostly images. Each Placido pattern comes with its "vectorial signature" — the biomechanical fingerprint it reveals.

Memorize these 5 patterns and you will know what to do in 80% of cases.

---

### Pattern 1: Central (Nipple)

**On Placido:** Small, round hot island (red) right in the center of the map.

**Vectorial Signature:**
- Biomechanical failure: **central and symmetric**
- Force field: radial centripetal (the protrusion pushes the center outward symmetrically)
- Implicit coma: **low** (the imbalance is already centered)

**Clinical Translation:** The problem is excessive curvature in the center. Needs **VR** (to flatten the center).

---

### Pattern 2: Inferior (Sagging / Oval)

**On Placido:** Oval red spot that "slides" to the lower half of the map. The map center might even be relatively "cold" (blue-green), while the bottom is "burning" (intense red).

**Vectorial Signature:**
- Biomechanical failure: **decentered inferiorly**
- Force field: asymmetric — pressure "won" in the inferior region where lamellae are weaker
- Implicit coma: **very high** (large distance between hot apex and pupillary center)

**Clinical Translation:** The problem is not just curvature — it is **position**. Needs **Vτ** (torque to reposition) more than VR.

---

### Pattern 3: Duck

**On Placido:** Two separate red foci, one superior and one inferior, with a green zone ("waist") between them. It looks like a duck's profile — head on top, body below.

**Vectorial Signature:**
- Biomechanical failure: **bimodal** — two points of weakness in opposite hemi-meridians
- Force field: two competing protrusion centers
- Implicit coma: **variable** (depends on which focus is larger)

**Clinical Translation:** Complex scenario. May require **two independent segments**, each treating one protrusion focus. Individualized vectorial analysis is mandatory.

---

### Pattern 4: Snowman

**On Placido:** Red spot extending vertically, resembling a snowman — wider at the bottom, narrower at the top. Unlike the Duck, the protrusion is continuous here (no green "waist" separating the foci).

**Vectorial Signature:**
- Biomechanical failure: **continuous vertical extension** with inferior > superior gradient
- Force field: elongated protrusion with inferior dominance
- Implicit coma: **moderate to high** (depends on how asymmetric the gradient is)

**Clinical Translation:** Combination of high VR (flattening over a long axis) + moderate Vτ (asymmetric gradient).

---

### Pattern 5: Regular Astigmatic (Bowtie)

**On Placido:** Two "hot" (red) opposite meridians with perpendicular "cold" (blue) meridians. The map looks like a bowtie or hourglass.

**Vectorial Signature:**
- Biomechanical failure: **meridional** — one meridian yielded more than the orthogonal one
- Force field: unequal tension between meridians (high on the flat axis, low on the steep axis)
- Implicit coma: **low** (if the pattern is symmetric and centered)

**Clinical Translation:** This is not keratoconus — it is corneal astigmatism. **VT** (tangential redistribution) is the dominant vector. If the astigmatism is high and centered, the ring acts by redistributing tension along the implanted meridian.
