## 🧠 BIOMECHANICAL VECTOR SUMMARY

### 1. VR (Radial Vector)
- **Primary Mechanism:** Arc-Shortening Effect & Barraquer's Law of Thickness.
- **Physical Action:** Centripetal force pulling peripheral cornea towards the center.
- **Clinical Result:** Central flattening.
- **Parameters:** Influenced by Ring Thickness / Diameter ratio.

### 2. VT (Tangential Vector)
- **Primary Mechanism:** Tensile Stress Redistribution.
- **Physical Action:** Managing stretching of stromal fibers along the ring path.
- **Clinical Result:** Stabilization of corneal shape; redistribution of apex-concentrated load.

### 3. Vτ (Torque Vector)
- **Primary Mechanism:** Rotational/Twisting forces.
- **Physical Action:** Local curvature transition; "bulking" effect.
- **Clinical Result:** Correction of irregular astigmatism; essential for Asymmetric Thickness ICRS models.
