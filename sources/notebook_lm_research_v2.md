## 🎯 CLINICAL & PHENOTYPE MAPPING

### 1. Phenotypes & Outcomes
- **Nipple (Central) Cones**: Responds best to **Symmetric Segments**. Provides uniform central flattening.
- **Sagging / Oval / Asymmetric Cones**: **Asymmetric (Progressive) Rings** are superior. Specifically reduces vertical **Coma** (VComa).
- **Visual Recovery**: Significant increase in BSCVA and reduction in K-max across all phenotypes when ring matches the phenotype.

### 2. Surgical & Technical Trends
- **Asymmetric Segments**: Progressive base width/thickness (e.g., 150 to 330 μm) allows customized flattening.
- **New Ferrara Nomogram**: Primary focus on **Corneal Asphericity (Q-value)** for ring selection.
- **Femtosecond Laser**: Preferred for tunnel creation. Standard depth: **70–80%** of corneal thickness.

### 3. Indications & Contraindications
- **Indications**: Mild to moderate keratoconus (Grades I-III), contact lens intolerance.
- **Minimum Thickness**: **350–450 μm** in the ring track area.
- **Contraindications**: Grade IV, apical scarring, active infection, pregnancy, thickness < 350 μm (extrusion risk).
